    The Twelve Pillars

    To summarize what I have learned from the book "The Twelve Pillars" I'll be using three points:

    Write Your Goals Down
    I have always had goals that I would like to acheive in life
    but it never once dawned on me that I should write them down.
    This book taught me that when you write your goals down it resonates
    in your mind and your brain begins thinking of ways to achieve them.
    This in turn makes it more likely for you to achieve those goals.
    As of now I will be applying this principle to my life.

    Surround yourself with the Best People
    In order to achieve your goals in the best way possible, you must
    surround yourself with people who are heading in the same direction as you. If you
    allow yourself to be surrounded by people who have no intention of developing themselves you
    will be stuck at the level you are at or sink even further. I've already began putting
    this principle into practice and it has been working very well for me.

    Be A Life Long Learner
    Some of us may think that after school ends and we've gotten our
    bachelors degree that we now know everything we need to know. But according to this book that
    idea could not be any further from the truth. It states that there is always new knowledge to gain and
    one of the best ways to do so is through reading. I too am a testament to the fact that reading
    has indeed improved my knowledge capacity so much so that I've been able to teach
    myself a lot of key skills that are important to my daily life.
